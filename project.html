Introduction

What drives audience preferences, and how have movie ratings evolved across genres over time? For making data-driven decisions, it is essential for the companies in the entertainment industry to understand the movie ratings and audience preferences. This analysis, conducted based on the dataset about popular movies from TMDb, delves into key indicators including movie vote averages, genres, release years. This study can be helpful for producers, marketers and movie platforms in shaping their content strategies. Nowadays, the companies including Disney+ and Netflix are rising in popularity. Therefore, analysing genre-specific trends in movie industry can be beneficial to reveal genres performing high consistently, preferences of audience and crucial shifts in ratings for these type of companies in terms of improving the quality of streaming services.

Data

The analysis is conducted based on open API from TMDb. It is a comprehensive dataset that has key variables like vote averages, release dates, genres and popularity scores. The data covers multiple decades which is very beneficial for getting a comprehensive view to audience preferences throughout provided timeframe.

Chart 6 | Movie Releases Across Months and Years (1990-2024)

This heatmap illustrates how many movies were released over the past three decades. The data is grouped by months and 5-year intervals. Higher number of movie releases are marked with darker shades of blue. The map shows that June-July is a peak period most probably because of summer holidays and more available time for audience to watch movies. December is also noted with the higher activity which can be explained by the holiday blockbusters. We can see the heat increasing in post-2010 period which can be associated with rising streaming platforms and lower production costs year by year. The technological advancements drives a sharp increase in movie production as we can see post 2000 period is marked with more movie releases.
